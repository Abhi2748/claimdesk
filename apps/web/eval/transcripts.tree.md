## Q19 (hard) — Missing required cite: I.G

### Question

I own a condo unit with this Dwelling Form policy, and my condo association also has an RCBAP. Can I collect under both policies for the same damage?

### Model answer

Based on the policy provisions, the answer depends on the specific circumstances, but generally your policy would be **excess** over the association's RCBAP, with an important exception and a hard cap.

---

**General Rule: Your Policy Is Excess**

If the condo association has flood insurance issued under the Act (i.e., an RCBAP) covering the same property, your Dwelling Form policy is in excess over that other insurance. [VII.B.2, p.15]

---

**Exception: Underinsured Association Policy**

Your policy can step in on a more primary basis if you receive a loss assessment from the association because the association's flood loss was **not fully reimbursed** under its RCBAP due to the building being insured for less than the lesser of:

- 80% or more of its full replacement cost [VII.B.2.a, p.15], or
- The maximum amount of insurance permitted under the Act [VII.B.2.b, p.16]

In that scenario, your policy would not simply remain excess.

---

**Hard Dollar Cap**

Regardless of how coverage is allocated between the two policies, the **combined payment cannot exceed $250,000 per single unit** under any circumstances. [VII.B.3, p.16]

---

In short, while both policies may ultimately contribute to covering your loss, your Dwelling Form policy is designed to sit behind the association's RCBAP, and total recovery is strictly capped at $250,000.
